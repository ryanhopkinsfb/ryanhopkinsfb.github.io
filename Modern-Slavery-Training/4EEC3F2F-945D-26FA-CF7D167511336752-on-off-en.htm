Best practice: Managing modern slavery risks

Here is a list of dos and don'ts to help manage modern slavery risks in our business:
  • Do
  • Always conduct due diligence checks on all workers, agencies, suppliers and third parties before engagement - it's vital that we know exactly who we are dealing with

  • Raise awareness of modern slavery and human trafficking among our suppliers and third parties – insist that they sign up to our Supplier Code of Conduct and insist on clauses in their contracts

  • Conduct enhanced due diligence on any supplier who is suspected or at high risk of using forced labour or child labour
  • Report immediately to your line manager, your local HR team or our confidential reporting hotline if you see or hear anything suspicious or have any concerns
  • Don't
  • Focus exclusively on specific groups or single out individuals based on their ethnicity or race when conducting due diligence - this is discriminatory and illegal
  • Waive our due diligence checks for those companies that already work with our partners or subsidiaries - separate ongoing checks are required for everyone
  • Directly challenge or confront those suspected of being involved in modern slavery or human trafficking, as you will tip them off and put victims in more danger - instead, report concerns via our recommended channels
